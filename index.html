List ordered · changeable · grabbed by position

Reach for a list when you have an ordered sequence of similar things and you mostly walk through the whole group or grab items by their position. A watchlist of tickers, a column of prices, the rows you loop over. You can add, drop, and reorder freely. 

watchlist = ['NVDA', 'AAPL', 'MSFT', 'AMZN', 'TSLA']

Dictionary looked up by name, not position

Reach for a dictionary the moment your instinct is “give me the value for AAPL” rather than “give me the third item.” Ticker to price, ticker to company, a setting to its value. It’s also exactly what an API hands back, since JSON is just a dictionary.

Click a ticker to look up its price:

prices['AAPL']  →  225

Tuple fixed · protected · travels together

Reach for a tuple when the values are fixed and should not change, or when a few values naturally travel together as one record: an FX or tax assumption, a coordinate, a row that should stay intact. Python locks a tuple on purpose, which protects it from a stray keystroke. 

fx = ('USD', 'EUR', 0.92)

Ask yourself: Am I looking this up by name? Dictionary. Is it an ordered list I’ll change? List. Should it never change? Tuple.
