List ordered · changeable · grabbed by position

Reach for a list when you have an ordered sequence of similar things and you mostly walk through the whole group or grab items by their position. A list of tickers, a column of prices, the rows you loop over. Add, drop, and reorder freely. 

prices = ['AAPL', 'MSFT', 'NVDA']

Dictionary looked up by name, not position

Reach for a dictionary the moment your instinct is “give me the value for AAPL” rather than “give me the third item.” Ticker to company, ticker to price, a setting to its value. This is also exactly what an API hands back — JSON is just a dictionary. 

companies['AAPL']  →  'Apple Inc.'

Tuple fixed · protected · travels together

Reach for a tuple when the values are fixed and should not change, or when a few values naturally travel together as one record — an FX or tax assumption, a coordinate, the multiple outputs a function returns. Python locks a tuple on purpose, which protects it from a stray keystroke. 

fx = ('USD', 'EUR', 0.92)

Ask yourself: Am I looking this up by name? Dictionary. Is it an ordered list I’ll change? List. Should it never change? Tuple.
